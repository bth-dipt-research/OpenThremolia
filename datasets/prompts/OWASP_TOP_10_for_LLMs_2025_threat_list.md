## OWASP Top 10 for LLM 2025:
LLM01: 2025 Prompt Injection - Manipulating LLMs via crafted inputs can lead to unauthorized access, data breaches, and compromised decision-making.
LLM02: 2025 Sensitive Information Disclosure - Failure to protect against disclosure of sensitive information in LLM outputs can result in legal consequences or a loss of competitive advantage.
LLM03: 2025 Supply Chain - Depending upon compromised components, services or datasets undermine system integrity, causing data breaches and system failures.
LLM04: Data and Model Poisoning - Manipulating training, fine-tuning, or embedding data introduces vulnerabilities, backdoors, or biases that degrade model security, performance, and ethical behavior.
LLM05: 2025 Improper Output Handling - Neglecting to validate LLM outputs may lead to downstream security exploits, including code execution that compromises systems and exposes data.
LLM06: 2025 Excessive Agency - Granting LLMs unchecked autonomy to take action can lead to unintended consequences, jeopardizing reliability, privacy, and trust.
LLM07: 2025 System Prompt Leakage - Exposing system prompts can reveal sensitive information, such as credentials or security controls, leading to potential attacks.
LLM08: 2025 Vector and Embedding Weaknesses - Insecure handling of vectors and embeddings in Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems can lead to data poisoning, model manipulation, and unauthorized access.
LLM09: 2025 Misinformation - LLMs can generate false or misleading information that appears credible, leading to security risks, reputational damage, and legal liability.
LLM10: 2025 Unbounded Consumption - Allowing excessive and uncontrolled LLM inferences can lead to denial of service (DoS), financial loss, model theft, and service degradation.
